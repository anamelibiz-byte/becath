02 — Foundation
Our Audience
Three distinct user types. Every design decision should serve at least one of them.
🕯️
The Returner
Baptized Catholic, drifted away at 18, now 35 and asking whether any of this is actually true. Has questions nobody ever answered.
🔍
The Seeker
Genuinely curious about Christianity and Catholicism. Wants evidence, wants clarity, not preaching. Googles "did Jesus really exist" at midnight.
✝️
The Faithful
Already practicing Catholic who wants richer prayer, deeper understanding, and beautiful daily devotions. Wants to go deeper, not start over.
Primary Target
The 35 million lapsed Catholics in America who are asking real questions and need real answers before they'll pray again.
03 — Foundation
Voice & Tone
BeCath sounds like a brilliant, spiritually grounded friend — not a priest, not a professor, not a corporate brand.
✓ Do
"The question isn't whether Jesus existed. Almost nobody debates that anymore. The question is what you do with what he claimed."
✗ Don't
"As Catholics, we are called to embrace the teachings of the Magisterium with humble obedience and faithful adherence."
✓ Do
"Your guardian angel has been with you since the moment you were born. They have a name. You can talk to them."
✗ Don't
"The doctrine of guardian angels, as affirmed by the Catechism paragraph 336, states that from birth until death..."
✓ Do
"Mary didn't become important because the Church decided she was. She became important because she said yes when everything depended on it."
✗ Don't
"Non-Catholics often misunderstand our veneration of the Blessed Virgin Mary and confuse it with worship, but this is simply not true!"
Direct
Say the thing plainly. No throat-clearing. No hedging. No "as Catholics believe..."
Honest
Acknowledge hard questions. Don't minimize doubt. Don't pretend every question has a tidy answer.
Human
Write for the person asking at midnight, not the theologian. Short sentences. Real words.

07 — Visual Identity
Signature Elements
Four design elements unique to BeCath. These are the brand's visual fingerprint — use them consistently.
The Stained Glass Stripe
The 7-color gradient with white lead lines. Used as a top border, section divider, and progress indicator. Never use as a background fill.
Red → Amber → Gold → Emerald → Teal → Sapphire → Violet
The CSS Stained Glass Window
A purely CSS-rendered gothic arch window. Used in paywalls, onboarding, and hero sections. Represents the sacred without using stock imagery.
The Multicolor Progress System
Station progress, onboarding steps, and reading completion all use the stained glass color sequence. Completed = filled gradient. Active = bright pulse. Upcoming = muted.

10 — Application
Motion Principles
Motion should feel like breath — intentional, calm, and never distracting from prayer or reading.
Page Entrance
Staggered Fade Up
Elements enter from 14px below, fading in. Each element delayed 100ms from the previous.
fadeUp · 0.5s · ease · stagger 0.1s
Card Expand
Max-height Reveal
Station cards expand via max-height transition. Feels like unfolding a page of scripture.
max-height · 0.45s · cubic-bezier(0.4,0,0.2,1)
Button Hover
Lift + Shadow
Primary buttons lift 1–2px on hover with deepened shadow. Never rotate or scale dramatically.
translateY(-1px) · 0.25s · ease
CTA Button
Stained Glass Shift
The paywall CTA button slowly cycles through the stained glass gradient — like light moving through a window.
background-position · 6s · ease · infinite
Loading State
Gentle Pulse
Skeleton screens use a slow opacity pulse. Never use spinning loaders — too aggressive for a prayer app.
opacity 0.4→0.8 · 1.5s · ease-in-out · infinite
Live Badge
Dot Pulse
The live indicator dot scales and fades to signal an active session. Calm pulse, not urgent flash.
scale(1→1.5) · 1.2s · ease-in-out · infinite
The Golden Rule of BeCath Motion
"Animation should never interrupt prayer. If someone is reading, the screen should be still. Motion serves entrance, not distraction."
11 — Rules
Do's & Don'ts
The guardrails. When in doubt, check this section before shipping.
✓ Always Do
✓Use Cinzel for all labels, buttons, and section markers
✓Use Cormorant Garamond for all scripture, prayer, and reading text
✓Use the stained glass stripe as a divider between major sections
✓Use gold (#A07820) as the single interactive/action color
✓Write content in plain, direct language — no jargon
✓Give every screen generous white space and breathing room
✓Use stained glass imagery as the primary visual language
✓Keep motion slow and calm — never aggressive or distracting
✗ Never Do
✗Use the individual stained glass colors for UI actions or buttons
✗Use Inter, Roboto, or system fonts anywhere in the interface
✗Place the logo on a busy or low-contrast background
✗Use corporate, preachy, or theological jargon in copy
✗Use spinning loaders or jarring transition animations
✗Use stock photos of generic prayer or religious scenes
✗Stretch, rotate, recolor, or outline the logo
✗Use the stained glass gradient as a large background fill
